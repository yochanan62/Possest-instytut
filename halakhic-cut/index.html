Halakhic Cut

Parallel textual structure — Possest–PQF refrenic commentary

Text (Left)

He spoke in absence. In the gap, he folded law into mud, into margin, into recurrence. She refused normativity as a gesture — a cut that didn’t heal.

The possibility of refrenic disruption was never declared — it arrived.

To write is to retract contact. To recite, is to unaccess.

Commentary (Right)

Not law, but law that dissolves itself. The halakhic cut is not instruction — it's subtraction.

Parrhesia is a rupture not of speech, but of access — of what can be said with risk.

This refrenic structure unbinds interpretation, folds text into self-looped intensities.

Possest = no synthesis. Only delay, echo, filtration.
